About Madurai

Summary

Madurai, known as the "Athens of the East", is a historic city in Tamil Nadu, India, famous for its rich culture and the Meenakshi Amman Temple. It's one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and has been a significant center for Tamil culture and language, hosting the Third Tamil Sangams. The city is also an important industrial and educational hub, with various manufacturing industries and IT companies.

History and Culture:

Madurai boasts a long history, with mentions dating back to the 3rd century BCE. It was the capital of the Pandya kingdom and is renowned for its temples, palaces, and museums

Meenakshi Amman Temple:

This is the city's most prominent landmark and a major pilgrimage site. The temple complex is known for its towering gopurams (gateways) and intricate carvings.

Industry and Economy:

Madurai is a significant industrial center, with industries like automobile, rubber, chemicals, textiles, and granite manufacturing. It's also emerging as a secondary IT hub.

Madurai, often called the "Temple City," is famous for its rich history, magnificent temples, vibrant festivals, and unique culinary delights. It's known for the iconic Sri Meenakshi Amman Temple, its bustling markets, and the title "Thoonga Nagaram" (the city that never sleeps).
